References

This theory book references the following key works, organized thematically.

Sandwich / Robust Variance Foundations

  • Binder (1983): On the variances of asymptotically normal estimators from complex surveys. International Statistical Review, 51(3), 279–292.
  • Liang & Zeger (1986): Longitudinal data analysis using generalized linear models. Biometrika, 73(1), 13–22.
  • MacKinnon & White (1985): Some heteroskedasticity-consistent covariance matrix estimators with improved finite sample properties. Journal of Econometrics, 29(3), 305–325.
  • Bell & McCaffrey (2002): Bias reduction in standard errors for linear regression with multi-stage samples. Survey Methodology, 28(2), 169–181.
  • Cameron & Miller (2015): A practitioner’s guide to cluster-robust inference. Journal of Human Resources, 50(2), 317–372.
  • Imbens & Kolesar (2016): Robust standard errors in small samples: Some practical advice. Review of Economics and Statistics, 98(4), 701–712.

Survey Methodology

  • Kish (1965): Survey Sampling. New York: Wiley.
  • Pfeffermann (1993): The role of sampling weights when modeling survey data. International Statistical Review, 61(2), 317–337.
  • Sarndal, Swensson, & Wretman (1992): Model Assisted Survey Sampling. New York: Springer-Verlag.
  • Rabe-Hesketh & Skrondal (2006): Multilevel modelling of complex survey data. JRSS-A, 169(4), 805–827.

Meta-Analysis Methodology

  • Hedges, Tipton, & Johnson (2010): Robust variance estimation in meta-regression with dependent effect size estimates. Research Synthesis Methods, 1(1), 39–65.
  • Tipton (2015): Small sample adjustments for robust variance estimation with meta-regression. Psychological Methods, 20(3), 375–393.
  • Pustejovsky & Tipton (2022): Meta-analysis with robust variance estimation: Expanding the range of working models. Prevention Science, 23, 425–438.
  • Pustejovsky & Chen (2024): Equivalencies between ad hoc strategies and multivariate models for meta-analysis of dependent effect sizes. JEBS, 49(6), 1013–1043.
  • Tanner-Smith & Lipsey (2015): Brief alcohol interventions for adolescents and young adults: A systematic review and meta-analysis. Journal of Substance Abuse Treatment, 51, 1–18.

Calibrated Bayes / Design-Based vs. Model-Based

  • Rubin (1984): Bayesianly justifiable and relevant frequency calculations for the applied statistician. The Annals of Statistics, 12(4), 1151–1172.
  • Little (2004): To model or not to model? Competing modes of inference for finite population sampling. JASA, 99(466), 546–556.
  • Little (2012): Calibrated Bayes, an alternative inferential paradigm for official statistics. Journal of Official Statistics, 28(3), 309–372.

Shrinkage / Small-Area Estimation Bridge

  • Efron & Morris (1975): Data analysis using Stein’s estimator and its generalizations. JASA, 70(350), 311–319.

Bayesian Survey Inference — Savitsky-Williams Stream

  • Savitsky & Toth (2016): Bayesian estimation under informative sampling. Electronic Journal of Statistics, 10(1), 1677–1708.
  • Williams & Savitsky (2020): Bayesian estimation under informative sampling with unattenuated dependence. Bayesian Analysis, 15(1), 57–77.
  • Williams & Savitsky (2021): Uncertainty estimation for pseudo-Bayesian inference under complex sampling. International Statistical Review, 89(1), 72–107.
  • Williams, McGuire, & Savitsky (2025): Uncertainty quantification for multi-level models using the survey-weighted pseudo-posterior. arXiv:2510.09401.

Bayesian Prior Distributions

  • Gelman (2006): Prior distributions for variance parameters in hierarchical models. Bayesian Analysis, 1(3), 515–534.

Author Self-Citations

  • Lee (2026a): A Bayesian hierarchical hurdle beta-binomial model for survey-weighted bounded counts. arXiv preprint, doi:10.48550/arXiv.2603.02611.
  • Lee (2026b): Design effect ratios for Bayesian survey models: A diagnostic framework. arXiv preprint, doi:10.48550/arXiv.2603.07791.

Software

  • Carpenter et al. (2017): Stan: A probabilistic programming language. Journal of Statistical Software, 76(1), 1–32.
